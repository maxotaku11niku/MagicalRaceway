Magical Raceway

UNDER CONSTRUCTION

This game is not finished yet!

Therefore, not all the content is in the game. Please do give feedback on these development versions.

Magical Raceway is a simple racing game that I developed solo. Inspired by classic arcade games, I hope it will be at least fun enough for short sessions. Unlike most racing games, though, it's about magical girls flying very quickly, though I don't think this is a revolutionary change since putting cute girls into stuff that didn't have them in the first place is so overdone. Like games such as OutRun, you race against the clock, so don't crash into anything!.

This game is available for Windows, Linux and Android. When this game is further into its development I will put it on more conventional platforms rather than hosting releases on GitHub, which means something like itch.io (not Steam: this game is too simple to justify being on Steam in my opinion) or Google Play.

What's in it?

Game Content

  • Five stages of racing against the clock, trying not to crash into other magical girls or stuff off the road!
  • Scoring system based on how quickly you complete the track, and also how risky you play.
  • Highscores are saved for later (but only locally, unfortunately). See if you can beat my scores!
  • No need for anything overcomplicated: it's short and simple, and doesn't demand you come back every day for some silly rewards. Contains absolutely no microtransactions nor adverts to distract you and drain your wallet.

Aesthetics

  • Pixellated graphics by yours truly meant to evoke the 16-bit era, with a pseudo-3D look that is meant to be consistent with what would have been possible with 1990s-era home systems. It's pretty cartoony, as well.
  • Set in a magical world, but clearly one in which technology (magitek) has advanced beyond even our world's level. Sufficiently Analysed Magic is in effect. This is my justification for the first stage looking like typical modern suburbia, among other things.
  • 95% genuine emulated Yamaha YM2608 FM soundchip sound. It's still chiptune, you know. All songs by yours truly.
